# P27 Quotient Automorphism Orbit Audit

Date: 2026-06-21

## Claim

The easy `F_p` automorphisms of

```text
C: b^2 = 16 - a^4
```

do not turn `T_line` into a cheap orbit selector.

They do clarify the structure:

```text
w -> -w        sends (a,b) -> (a,-b)
t -> -1/t     sends (a,b) -> (a,b)
t -> 1/t      sends (a,b) -> (-a,b)
t -> -t       sends (a,b) -> (-a,b)
```

The domain bit is invariant under the `a -> -a` automorphisms, but `T_line` is
not governed by any exact degree-2 product of the named branch signs tested
here.

Exact positives:


For the `a -> -a` maps, `T_line` is balanced rather than determined:


The best degree-2 branch-sign products match the `T_line` `a -> -a` ratio only
about three quarters of the time, not exactly:

```text
main top matches ~= 12094 / 16096
held-out top matches ~= 12370 / 16362
```

This is too weak to promote as a theorem or sampler.

## Interpretation

Positive:

```text
The domain bit is invariant under the full easy F_p quotient automorphism
orbit generated by b -> -b and a -> -a.
The prior Hilbert-90 identity under t -> -1/t is consistent with this audit.
```

Negative:

```text
T_line is invariant under b -> -b and t -> -1/t, but not under a -> -a.
No exact degree-2 branch-sign ratio explains T_line across the a -> -a orbit.
The easy F_p automorphism group does not yield a sqrt-beating orbit sampler.
```
