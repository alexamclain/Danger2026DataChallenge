# Trace-GCD Chow Special-Divisor Frontier

Date: 2026-06-06

This note sharpens the Borcherds/local-intersection search from “find a
product formula” to “find an honest special divisor for the trace-GCD Chow
section.”

## Credible Special-Divisor Criterion

A useful Borcherds/Siegel/unit formula for the trace-GCD Chow determinant
must satisfy all of the following.

```text
1. Vector honesty:
   it matches the actual right-phase Chow vector
     (Delta(t))_t
   or a proven orbit/global/full-origin norm of that vector, not merely an
   unrelated scalar with the same final value.

2. Divisor honesty:
   its divisor is the pulled-back translated Schubert/Chow divisor, possibly
   plus boundary or vertical terms that are p-units at the selected p24 prime.

3. Non-random recognition:
   in small actual-CM rows, the target should lie in a special-unit/divisor
   span before that span becomes the whole ambient phase-vector space.

4. p-local unit theorem:
   at p = 10^24 + 7, the local intersection with the selected ordinary CM
   point is zero at one of the explicit orientations
     sqrt(D_K)=+/-t/2 mod p.
```

The scalar-only global product miner fails item 1 as evidence: it finds
low-weight scalar matches that do not lift to the right-phase vector.

## Expanded Unit-Span Test

The bounded special-divisor dictionary currently contains:

```text
right-binomial cyclotomic units:
  Norm(1 - c*zeta_right^(k*t));

small Heegner-fiber units:
  product over non-right origins with fixed right phase of (j_i - h),
  where h is a reduction of a small Heegner root.
```

The expanded pinned-row run was:

  --max-origin-shifts 140 \

## Consequence

The easy special-divisor explanation is now demoted in a stronger bounded
window:

```text
plain j / one-edge divisor: negative;
small Heegner support in plain j: negative;
bounded phase-unit span: negative;
global scalar product recognition: misleading without vector/divisor match.
```

This does not disprove a Borcherds proof.  It says that the needed product, if
it exists, is a bespoke phase-aware Schubert/Fitting divisor, not a product
generated by the small cyclotomic and Heegner-fiber units tested here.

The surviving theorem target is therefore:

```text
construct a p-integral phase-aware Chow/Fitting/Borcherds section Psi_trace
whose divisor is the pulled-back translated Schubert divisor and whose p24
local intersection is zero at the explicit ordinary prime above p.
```

That target is exactly what the finite gates consume; it remains the missing
arithmetic theorem.
