# Hermitian Mixed Leading-Erasure Theorem

This note records the current sharpest finite theorem candidate after the
Delsarte/delete-one and Moore reductions.

## p24 Right-Orbit Order

For `p=10^24+7`, the nonzero right frequencies modulo `211` split into six
Frobenius orbits of length `35`.  With the natural smallest-representative
order they are:

```text
O_1 rep 1
O_2 rep 2
O_3 rep 4
O_4 rep 8
O_5 rep 16
O_6 rep 29
```

The left side has one orbit modulo `157` of length `156`.

Let

```text
a_j(lambda) = Tr_{E/R}(lambda*S_j) in R=F_p(mu_211)
```

and choose the Lang/trace-dual coordinate order within each `a_j` block.  The
full Delsarte coordinate map is:

```text
Phi(lambda) = (a_1(lambda),...,a_6(lambda)) in R^6 ~= F_p^210.
```

The delete-one theorem asks that after removing any one `R`-block, the
remaining `175` coordinates still separate all nonzero `lambda in L`.

## Leading-Erasure Form

The leading Moore candidate strengthens this to a named coordinate window.
For each deleted block `j`, keep the remaining blocks in the original orbit
order and take the first `156` scalar coordinates.  Since

```text
156 = 4*35 + 16,
```

the omitted coordinates are:

```text
all 35 coordinates of O_j,
plus the final 19 coordinates of the last kept right block.
```

Thus failure of the leading theorem is equivalent to:

```text
exists lambda != 0 such that Phi(lambda) lies in a 54-dimensional erasure
subspace E_j.
```

Here `E_j` is:

```text
j != 6:  O_j full block  +  tail_19(O_6)
j  = 6:  O_6 full block  +  tail_19(O_5)
```

The p24 certificate candidate is therefore the six incidence avoidances:

```text
Phi(L) ∩ E_j = {0},      j=1,...,6.
```

This is equivalent to six leading `156 x 156` Moore determinants being
nonzero, or equivalently six residual norm products being p-units.

## Full-Block Plus Tail Split

The leading window has a useful internal shape:

```text
156 = 4*35 + 16.
```

For each deleted orbit `j`, let the kept orbits be ordered as usual.  The
first four kept `R`-blocks contribute `140` full Lang coordinates.  The fifth
kept block contributes only its first `16` coordinates.

So each leading Moore determinant can be checked by two nested p-unit claims:

```text
rank(first four kept blocks) = 140,
rank(first four kept blocks + first 16 of fifth block) = 156.
```

Dually, the first four kept right traces cut `L` down to an expected
`16`-dimensional residual subspace, and the first `16` scalar coordinates of
the fifth kept trace separate that residual.  This is the smallest current
proof target:

```text
K_j = intersection of the four full right-trace kernels,  dim K_j = 16;
tail_16 on the last kept orbit restricts injectively to K_j.
```

The actual-CM audit reports this split as:

```text
delete_one_prefix_full_block_counts
delete_one_prefix_tail_lengths
delete_one_prefix_full_block_min_rank
delete_one_prefix_tail_min_augmentation
delete_one_prefix_full_block_norm_products_base
delete_one_prefix_tail_norm_products_base
```

For p24 the desired values are:

```text
full_block_count = 4
tail_length = 16
full_block_rank = 140
tail_augmentation = 16
```

In subspace-polynomial terms, first compute the annihilator `P_j` of the four
full kept blocks.  This gives a prefix p-unit

```text
B_j = product_{a=0}^{139} Norm_{L/F_p}(P_{j,a}(b_{j,a+1})).
```

Then the quotient-tail p-unit is:

```text
T_j = product_{a=0}^{15} Norm_{L/F_p}(Q_{j,a}(tail_{j,a+1})),
```

where the incremental polynomials `Q_{j,a}` start from `P_j` rather than from
`X`.  Nonzero `B_j` proves the first four kept blocks have rank `140`, and
nonzero `T_j` proves that the tail supplies the missing `16` dimensions.
Together they are the factorized form of the larger leading `156 x 156` Moore
determinant.

## Explicit p24 Manifest

The exact orbit/factor manifest is generated by:

```text
p24/p24_factorized_certificate_manifest.py
```

The six deleted-orbit rows are:

```text
deleted O1: B={O2,O3,O4,O5}, T=first16(O6)
deleted O2: B={O1,O3,O4,O5}, T=first16(O6)
deleted O3: B={O1,O2,O4,O5}, T=first16(O6)
deleted O4: B={O1,O2,O3,O5}, T=first16(O6)
deleted O5: B={O1,O2,O3,O4}, T=first16(O6)
deleted O6: B={O1,O2,O3,O4}, T=first16(O5)
```

Thus there are only five distinct four-block prefix factors `B_j`; the
`{O1,O2,O3,O4}` prefix is shared by the `O5` and `O6` deletion rows.  There
are still six quotient-tail factors, because the shared kernel is tested
against two different tail blocks `O6` and `O5`.

The natural order is not the only legal choice of `156` coordinates after a
deletion.  Pairing the six deleted orbits gives a smaller prefix-factor list:

```text
deleted O1: B={O3,O4,O5,O6}, T=first16(O2)
deleted O2: B={O3,O4,O5,O6}, T=first16(O1)
deleted O3: B={O1,O2,O5,O6}, T=first16(O4)
deleted O4: B={O1,O2,O5,O6}, T=first16(O3)
deleted O5: B={O1,O2,O3,O4}, T=first16(O6)
deleted O6: B={O1,O2,O3,O4}, T=first16(O5)
```

This uses only three distinct prefix factors `B`.  That is optimal at the
level of four-block certificates: one four-block prefix excludes exactly two
orbits, so it can serve at most two deletion rows; hence at least
`ceil(6/2)=3` distinct `B` factors are necessary.  The six tail factors remain
row-specific.

A random linear paired-tail toy confirms that the two tail factors sharing one
prefix are independent finite-linear obligations.  In the toy, there are many
rows where the shared prefix has full rank and exactly one of the two tail
maps is full.  Thus the six `T` factors cannot be compressed by linear
algebra alone.

## Symmetry-Aligned Opposite Pairing

There is a better pairing for proof search than the adjacent pairing above.
For p24,

```text
v -> -v mod 211
```

permutes the six right Frobenius orbits by

```text
O1 <-> O4,
O2 <-> O5,
O3 <-> O6.
```

Pairing opposite orbits gives another optimal three-prefix certificate:

```text
deleted O1/O4 share B={O2,O3,O5,O6}, tails O4/O1
deleted O2/O5 share B={O1,O3,O4,O6}, tails O5/O2
deleted O3/O6 share B={O1,O2,O4,O5}, tails O6/O3
```

Each shared prefix is stable under `v -> -v`.  In the full cyclotomic product
algebra, inversion therefore reduces the arithmetic tail proof from six
unrelated `T` factors to three conjugacy classes of `T` factors:

```text
T_{1 -> 4} conjugate to T_{4 -> 1},
T_{2 -> 5} conjugate to T_{5 -> 2},
T_{3 -> 6} conjugate to T_{6 -> 3}.
```

This does not follow from ordinary linear algebra alone; it uses the
cyclotomic inversion automorphism on the full product algebra.  The mixed
Hermitian periods and the chosen length-16 tail coordinate windows must be
stated in compatible Lang coordinates.
The natural smallest-representative orbit order does not make the first-16
windows conjugate; the opposite-pair manifest therefore uses rotated
q-consecutive tail windows, e.g. the `O4` tail window is the negation of the
chosen `O1` window.
There is also a raw-seed/Lang-coordinate distinction.  Inversion sends

```text
H(1,p^k v) -> H(-1,-p^k v),
```

and `-1 = p^78 mod 157`, so the raw seed row contains the semilinear shift
`T^78`.  After Lang trivialization this shift becomes coordinatewise
Frobenius on the transformed coordinates, not a coordinate-index shift.  Thus
the conjugation-compatible tail windows are the direct negation windows in
Lang coordinates, not the raw shifted seed positions.  The convention is
checked by:

```text
p24/opposite_conjugation_window_audit.py
```

which reports `lang_shift_coordinate_mismatches=0` in small finite-field
models and prints the p24 raw-seed versus Lang-window offsets.
The finite implication is isolated in:

```text
p24/lean/ConjugateTailGate.lean
```

The finite algebra theorem and coordinate convention are recorded in:

```text
p24/opposite_conjugation_tail_theorem.md
```

The next proof target is consequently sharper:

```text
prove 3 opposite-prefix p-units B,
prove 3 representative opposite-tail p-units T.
```

The right-unit action on `F_p[mu_211]` sharpens this again.  Multiplication by
`2 mod 211` cycles the six right factors and the three opposite prefixes.  In
an equivariant full-product-algebra formulation, this reduces the arithmetic
classes to:

```text
prove 1 representative leading Moore p-unit L = B*T.
```

The unit-action audit and theorem are recorded in:

```text
p24/representative_leading_punit_certificate.md
p24/right_orbit_unit_action_audit.py
p24/right_unit_equivariance_theorem.md
p24/lean/UnitOrbitGate.lean
```

If a verifier insists on the natural smallest-representative tail windows
instead of the compatible Lang windows, the opposite pairing remains a valid
`3B+6T` certificate but gives no tail compression.

## Why This Helps

The previous theorem was existential:

```text
some 156 of 210 coordinates form a Moore p-unit.
```

The leading-erasure form names the six exact p-units.  It also turns the
dual obstruction into a low-dimensional incidence problem:

```text
156-dimensional CM trace image Phi(L)
avoids six explicit 54-dimensional erasure subspaces of F_p^210.
```

A purely generic dimension count is not a proof, but it gives the right
shape: a random `156`-space in `210` dimensions misses a fixed `54`-space
with codimension-one slack.  The missing arithmetic theorem is that the CM
image has the same avoidance for these six structured erasures at the p24
prime.

The random mixed-orbit toy is an important caution.  In the six-right-orbit
miniature, delete-one rank held in all tested rows, but leading-prefix rank
held in only `10/80` rows.  So this leading-erasure theorem is not a formal
consequence of the Delsarte decomposition or of random delete-one rank; it is
a named CM p-unit target.
