# Lang Trace-GCD Crossed-Weight Spectral Boundary

Date: 2026-06-05

## Question

The crossed-product orbit norm packages the determinant values correctly:

```text
Pi_O = prod_{t in O} Delta(t).
```

Could the weights `Delta(t)` themselves be generated by a low-order
recurrence or a one-orbit Fourier support theorem?  If yes, the producer might
construct a much smaller spectral object instead of the seven orbit norms.

## p24 Comparison

The same audit prints the p24 exterior-support comparison:

```text
p24_right=211
p24_p_mod_right=114
p24_orbit_len=35
p24_tail=16
distinct_subset_sum_size_k1=35
distinct_subset_sum_size_k2=210
distinct_subset_sum_size_k3=211
distinct_subset_sum_size_k16=211
full_support_by_k3=1
```

Thus a p24 determinant of tail size `16` can have full Fourier support in
`Z/211Z` already by representation theory.  A one-orbit support theorem for
p24 would require special CM/Plucker cancellation:

```text
c_s =
  sum_{I subset O, |I|=16, sum(I)=s}
    det(P_I) det(A_I)

must vanish on 176 of the 211 exponents.
```

That is a genuine arithmetic theorem candidate, not a formal consequence of
the crossed-product or exterior-power setup.

## Consequence

The current producer theorem should not be downgraded to a low-recurrence
certificate unless one first proves this special cancellation.

Safe current target:

```text
construct seven crossed-product orbit norms Pi_O and prove they are p-units.
```

Stronger but unproved target:

```text
prove f_trace has support on one degree-35 Frobenius orbit of exponents,
then prove the corresponding degree-35 norm is a p-unit.
```

The small right-7 row supports the possibility, but the p24 support audit
says it is not generic and should not be assumed.
