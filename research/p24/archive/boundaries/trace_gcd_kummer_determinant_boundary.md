# Trace-GCD Kummer Determinant Boundary

Date: 2026-06-05

This note closes one tempting but too-weak version of the direct Kummer route.

## False Shortcut

The finite Kummer bridge proves:

```text
K_s = T_s^r is a p-unit  =>  T_s is nonzero.
```

It does **not** prove that every determinant built from the `T_s` is nonzero.
The trace-GCD certificate needs a determinant:

```text
f_trace(zeta_211^t) = det(P V_t A),
```

or equivalently a Plucker/Fitting coordinate of a trace-dual subspace.  A
determinant may vanish by linear dependence even when every entry is nonzero.

```text
all_entries_nonzero=1

```

So coordinatewise Kummer p-units are insufficient for trace-GCD determinant
nonvanishing.

## Correct Theorem Shape

The surviving direct Kummer theorem must be one of:

```text
Plucker-Kummer theorem:
  the specific trace-GCD Plucker coordinate is itself a p-unit polynomial
  in relative Kummer data;

Fitting-Kummer theorem:
  the Fitting ideal generated by the Kummer/resolvent coordinates contains
  the trace-GCD determinant, or detects its zero locus;

determinantal zero-detection theorem:
  det(P V_t A)=0 forces at least one named Kummer/Plucker payload to vanish.
```

The last form is the finite interface already named in:

```text
p24/trace_gcd_kummer_nonvanishing_bridge.md
p24/lean/KummerNonvanishingGate.lean
```

But the payload cannot be merely "all primitive relative traces are nonzero."
It must include enough exterior/Plucker information to rule out cancellation.

## Consequence

The direct `f_trace` construction route remains alive, but it has been
sharpened:

```text
construct f_trace or its Plucker/Fitting ideal in relative Kummer coordinates,
not just the coordinatewise relative Kummer p-units.
```

This aligns with the operator-norm formulation:

```text
f_trace = det_Q(P V_univ A)
```

is the object to construct.  Kummer powers are a useful coordinate system for
the embedded class-field phase, not by themselves the determinant certificate.
