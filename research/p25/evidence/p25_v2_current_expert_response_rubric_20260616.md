# P25 v2 Current Expert Response Rubric

Updated: 2026-06-17

## Purpose

Consolidate the live p25 v2 contracts into one response classifier for expert
answers, paper snippets, or source-search reports.  This page does not claim
the missing theorem exists.  It says how to route an answer against the current
target, row normalizer, conductor-39 selector rigidity, value-branch contract,
Koo-Shin Theorem 5.2 obstruction, row orientation, source-snippet intake,
DANGER3 finite-identity framing, and extraction payload contract. It also
guards against reversing the exact-P-to-unified spine without the missing
exact-P selector data, and classifies broad quadratic aggregate, row-quotient
bridge, row-square, or unspecified `F_p^*`-scalar claims as repair unless
they recover one normalized oriented sparse `W`-boundary value. It also applies
the quartic selector payload: character-language answers need the exact
row-antisymmetric `C4_1` phase, not just phase sign, magnitude, quadratic data,
or boundary-visible data, before they can select one edge. It also
applies the quartic reciprocal-orientation screen: reciprocal rows negate both
the Hilbert-90 boundary and `C4_1` phase, so phase collisions with the
opposite oriented edge are repair rows and reciprocal phase with positive
boundary is rejected. It also
classifies dense period-norm-only claims as repair unless they include legal
Hilbert-90 descent to one support-156 row, and degree-6 value/orbit claims as
repair unless they explicitly descend to `F_p` and select one legal row. It
also separates conductor-39 source identification, Yang lift, H90 boundary,
and finite theorem content, and classifies additive normalization,
coefficient-6 root normalization, p25 power/scalar ambiguity, and powered
output-kind claims. It also routes source-graph edge and edge-lattice
combination claims through the current one-edge target rule, and routes
canonical H0/Y507 period-156 value claims through the promoted compatibility
screen. It also applies the H90 support lower bound: a support-below-12
preimage of the current `W` is impossible, while support-12 claims must be one
of the four legal mixed minimizers before they can enter theorem intake. It
also routes exact-P leads through the minimal exact-P hook: KSY vocabulary,
raw Kubert-Lang balance, branchless orientation words, theta2 values without
period-156 context, wrong packets, nonuniform atoms, and ambient-period
value-only claims remain repair or reject rows. It also routes the compact
conductor-39 norm-one quotient `Q = prod_{h in <2>} E_{7h}/E_h`: `Q` value
theorems with period-156 context and finite `Q^3` Hilbert-90 preimage theorems
are support routes; source-only, boundary-only, primitive-power-only, and pure
degree-6 norm claims are repair or reject rows. It also applies the Q
diagonal-normalization screen: Q-only diagonal value data remains support,
Q plus row-quotient data is a square-root repair, Q plus explicit oriented
diagonal split normalizes to one-edge intake, and wrong zero-boundary splits
are rejected.

## 2026-06-17 Sync Note

This rubric remains the broad expert-answer router, but source-stage theorem
classification now defers to the source theorem acceptance automaton. Two
later updates supersede older rubric rows:

```text
unique row powers = e in {3,5,13,39,75,169,507}
distribution/norm aggregate closure = repair_even_boundary_distribution_closure
matched aggregate route = normalize_matched_quotient_then_accept
```

So an exact row-labeled `R_m^75`, `R_m^169`, or `R_m^507` theorem is accepted
by inverse exponent when it includes source theorem provenance, finite payload,
row label, and boundary/period bridge. Generic distribution, norm, vertex-sum,
diagonal, all-four aggregate, or boundary-zero quotient relation data remains
repair until it supplies direct edge data or root/selector/scalar
normalization.

The aggregate repair can now promote only in the matched form: exact `R^v`
source theorem plus exact matched zero-lattice value
`R^(v - (sum v)e_m)`, with `gcd(sum v,p-1)=1`.
