- No finite value/divisor theorem for the legal support-156 H90/Yang product is
  in hand.
- The Yang lift and Hilbert-90 boundary are finite targets, not a source-stage
  close by themselves.
- Mixed `U_chi/W` source identification without Yang lift, Yang lift without
  H90/descent boundary, and Yang/H90 source without a finite theorem are all
  repair rows, not source-stage closers.
- A compact `Q` quotient answer must still supply period-156 context and a
  finite theorem for `Q`, `Q^3`, `Q^6`, or the selected Yang lift; coset
  selector language alone is not enough.
- Even with finite `Q` or `Q^3` theorem data, source-stage promotion still
  needs the order-4 quotient-`C4` edge selector, the boundary-zero normalization
  to one edge, or a direct one-edge theorem.

  `Q_antisym=m1+m4=m2+m8`. A diagonal aggregate plus a boundary-zero quotient
  reaches only twice one edge until a source supplies oriented root/sign data.
- The global classifiers now enforce that exact Q distinction; a future answer
  cannot promote through this lane unless it supplies the oriented split/root
  data or a direct one-edge theorem.
- Support-12 row quotient answers are not enough for the Q diagonal route:
  the exact Q split is `m1-m4` or `m2-m8`, and both use all twelve mod-13
  columns.
- The exact Q split has pure quartic selector structure, but Q diagonal plus
  correct pure quartic split still reaches a row square rather than a submitted
  one-edge value unless an oriented root/sign or direct one-edge theorem is
  supplied.
- An exact scalar-fixed Q-square value is nevertheless operationally useful:
  it gives two `F_p` row roots. It is not extraction-ready until a map to
  same-j/X_1(16)/halving data or concrete `(A,x0)` candidates is supplied.
  Divisor, boundary, phase, and value-up-to-scalar statements do not give that
