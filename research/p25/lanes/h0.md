# H0

## Purpose

Track the first-pass H0 source-theorem lane and keep its closing shape compact.

## Current Claim

H0 is one of the two best first-pass moonshot targets. The source-legality side
is real: Koo-Shin 2010 and the exact H0 translate screens certify the relevant
H0 products as legal source objects.

The legal objects have now been compressed to exactly four 78-over-78
H0/H0-translate products in the doubling orbit. What is still missing is one
of two closers:

```text
one exact H0 product
+ arithmetic source theorem
+ finite period-156 value theorem
+ boundary (1 - Frob_p) H0 = Norm_156(Y_507)
```

or:

```text
one exact H0 product
+ arithmetic source theorem
+ finite divisor/additive theorem
+ Hilbert-90 boundary to Norm_156(Y_507)
```

Without that theorem, H0 remains a source-certified lane, not a submission
route.

For new work, the positive row is narrow: an exact legal H0 product plus
theorem body, arithmetic source, and either period-156 value data or
divisor/additive output with the Hilbert-90 boundary. Source certification
alone, value-only language without period-156 context, or a missing H90
boundary is a repair row, not a win.

The H0 finite targets are now known to be identical to the conductor-39 legal
sparse Yang/H90 products. H0 remains a useful source language, but it should
not be counted as an independent finite theorem target separate from conductor
39.

The source-family gap matrix keeps the H0 literature ask narrow. Koo-Shin 2010
is the closest inspected source family because it has the one-edge source
object vocabulary, but it still lacks the scalar-fixed finite value/divisor
theorem or period-156 value theorem needed to close this lane.

## Open Blockers

- No exact finite H0 value/divisor theorem is in hand.
- The H90 / period-156 boundary is still missing from the source-stage closer.
- H0 and conductor 39 share the same finite target family, so new H0 work must
  add a theorem or source route, not merely rediscover the conductor-39 product.
- A conductor-39-language restatement of the H0 target is repair unless it
  supplies the level-507 Yang lift, H90 boundary, and finite theorem content
  required by the shared target.
- There is no remaining useful gauge/selector freedom in the shared finite
  target under current evidence.
- A value-only H0 theorem is acceptable only with period-156 branch/root/
  telescoping context; a divisor/additive theorem with H90 boundary is the
  preferred row.
- H0 value-side source answers should pass through the period-156 value source
  hook and H0/Y507 compatibility screen before source-stage promotion.
- H0 period-156 value answers should also satisfy the row bridge packet:
  finite value/additive payload, arithmetic source, period-156 normalization,
  legal row bridge, `Norm_156(Y_507)` boundary, and scalar/branch
  normalization over `F_p`.
- H0 period-value, H0/Y507, ambient-period, degree-6, norm-only, theta2, or
  Schertz/Shin/Scholl value-side replies should pass through the period-156
  value candidate sweep before source-stage promotion.
- An ambient-period-780 value theorem, 11th-power or `mu_11` quotient theorem,
  degree-6 value without explicit `F_p` descent and selected-row data, direct
  `F_p` order-39-root shortcut, or `sqrt(-39)` scalar shortcut is now a
  repair/reject row under the period-156 and degree-6 descent contracts.
- Even a source-stage win still has to route through DANGER3 framing, same-`j`
  `X_1(8112)`, X1(16) extraction, halving, and official verification.
- The downstream ladder is defined but no live H0 payload currently supplies a
  same-`j` bridge, practical `A,xP16`, halving chain, direct `x0`, or verified
  p25 triple.
- Independent level-16 and level-507 data are not enough for H0 extraction
  without same-j gluing.
- Matching `j` or an isomorphism class is still not enough for H0 extraction
  without same-curve torsion data, an order-8112 generator, or direct
  `A,xP16`.
- Each H0 pass must end with either a source-stage closing candidate matching
  the positive row, or a falsifier for the attempted theorem shape.
- Broad source rereads are not a lane action; a source lead must already point
  to the accepted theorem row or explain a new falsifier.
- The review packet is the supporting handoff surface for expert feedback; an
  H0 lead should answer one of its five questions or kill the stated theorem
  shape.
- The self-contained theorem statement is now the first page to send or use for
  H0 expert intake; the review packet and group-ring payload are supporting
  layers behind it.
- In source language, one H0 theorem should now be read as a theorem for one
  oriented quotient-`C4` graph edge; graph vertices, edge quotients, diagonal
  pairs, row squares, or all-four aggregates still need selector/orientation
  repair.
- H0 answers stated as integer combinations of the four edge rows should pass
  the edge-lattice classifier: coefficient sum `1` gives the right boundary
  scale, but a non-unit vector is still edge plus boundary-zero content.
- H0 boundary-zero quotient answers can transfer or normalize a row theorem
  only after exact scalar-fixed zero-lattice values are supplied; quotient data
  alone is not the first source-stage close.
- H0 quotient, diagonal, row-square, nonunit `W`-boundary, or Q-square answers
  should pass through the zero-lattice candidate sweep before source-stage
  promotion.
- H0 aggregate row-product answers promote only through the matched-quotient
  closure packet: exact `R^v`, exact matched `R^(v - (sum v)e_m)`, and
  invertible coefficient sum.
- H0 product claims should match the group-ring payload row exactly; a different
  exponent row is a new claim, not the current target.
- H0 row presentations should be normalized by the row-orbit contract before
  intake; outside-doubling-orbit multipliers are not current legal rows.
- Outside-doubling H0 presentations are reciprocal rows only with explicit
  reciprocal orientation and `-Norm_156(Y_507)` boundary sign; without that
  sign they are repair rows, and reciprocal rows with positive boundary are
  rejected.

  all-four product are repair/context only unless they also select or factor to
  one sparse legal row with the original `W` boundary.

  H90 boundary, and aggregate-plus-quotient reaches a row square rather than a
  selected row without extra halving/root/orientation data.

  rows unless they also name the oriented root or prove the one-row theorem
  directly.
- H0 lower-coefficient root answers are usable only after powering back to the

  target statements unless they prove that row directly.

  rejected.
