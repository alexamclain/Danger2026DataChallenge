# Koo-Shin 2010

## Purpose

Summarize the actual role of the 2010 Math. Z. paper now that the PDF and text
extract are available locally.

## Current Claim

Koo-Shin 2010 is now a real source in hand, and it matters. Its strongest p25
use is source legality and context:

- Theorem 6.2 certifies compact conductor-39 and exact H0 source words as legal
  Siegel-function products.
- Theorem 5.2 and related clauses give root-descent and rigidity context.
- The paper does **not** currently supply the missing p25 finite value/divisor
  theorem that would close source stage.
- The constructive-payload scan also finds no exact p25 row value, product
  packet, period-156 payload, or DANGER3 extraction data in the local extract.
- The old external-scout access action is closed: the PDF/OCR problem has been
  superseded by the v2 local-source scans, and the remaining blocker is a
  missing theorem rather than source retrieval.

So this paper is a source-certification asset, not yet a closer.

## Open Blockers

- No clause from the paper currently closes H0 or conductor 39 at source stage.
- The missing closer is still a finite value/divisor theorem with the right
  boundary data, not more source legality.
- Theorem 5.2's constant-product rigidity does not match the four current
  nonconstant quotient-`C4` legal rows; Lemma 6.1 and Theorem 6.2 do not supply
  finite p25 values by themselves.
- Nor does any nontrivial product of powers of the four legal rows become a
  Theorem 5.2 constant-exponent product; the legal quotient-`C4` span only
  meets the constant line at zero.
- The local extract does not currently contain the additive-normalization
  closer words now demanded by the v2 contract: basepoint/telescoping,
  period-156/H90/Y507, or scalar-fixing finite value data for one legal row.
- The local extract also lacks the constructive payload hooks now demanded by
  packet intake: exact p25 row value, exact product packet, period-156 payload,
  or DANGER3 extraction data.
- The local extract also lacks the exact conductor-39 `Q` route hooks now
  demanded by the Q support route.
- There is no remaining Koo-Shin access blocker to resolve before assessing
  the current source-stage gap.
- The first priority-1 lookup row is now falsified for Koo-Shin 2010 itself:
  the missing scalar-fixed divisor/additive theorem is not hidden in the
  already-screened 2010 clauses.
- For H0, that means a theorem for one legal H0/H0-translate product with
  period-156 value data or a Hilbert-90 divisor/additive boundary.
- For conductor 39, that means a theorem for the legal support-156
  Hilbert-90/Yang product, not another statement that merely certifies the
  source word.
