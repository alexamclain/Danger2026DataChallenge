# Kubert-Lang

## Purpose

Capture the current KL boundary for the mixed exact-product route.

## Current Claim

Kubert-Lang remains live as exact exponent/dependence machinery, and the finite
p25 selector boundary is now sharp: the exact packet passes the necessary KL
exponent-matrix screen, the primitive word is the rigid bridge word
`z^121 * (1 + z + z^2) * (1 - z^263)`, and the `C_3 x C_169` quotient selector
is rigid up to orientation and D-reversal.

This is positive finite structure, not a direct closer on its own. A KL-style
source result still needs to emit the exact mixed selector, primitive word, or
accepted theta2/theta2-inverse payload as an arithmetic theorem.

The external source boundary now adds the web-source status. KL IV and
`Modular Units` are the right source anchors for generator theory, and modern
summaries are useful for theorem-K-style congruence language. None of that
generic framework is enough for p25 unless it emits the rigid primitive word,
the oriented mixed `C_3 x C_169` selector, or an accepted theta2 payload.

The primitive-word source split now gives that ask a second source-facing
form: the normalized word is `(1+z+z^2)*(1-z^263)`, equivalently
`(1-z^3)*(1-z^263)/(1-z)`, and the same bridge can be reached from the
three-term Hilbert-90 source chain `-(1+z+z^-121)` with unique primitive
boundary step `122` up to sign. This is still support unless a source theorem
also supplies p25 orientation and K-trace/theta2 bridge data.

The local-source scan checks that sharper hook against the raw local KSY,
Sprang, Koo-Shin 2010, and Koo-Shin II extracts. Those sources still have
useful helper vocabulary, but no exact primitive word, H90 source chain,
boundary step, or K-trace/theta2 bridge as a source theorem.

The cyclotomic-norm route has now been separated from that exact-P hook.
Kubert-Lang modular units inside cyclotomic units and Robert-unit module
relations are genuine theorem-level support, but they do not select one p25
support-156 row, do not name `Norm_156(Y_507)`, and do not emit
`C,D,K,orientation`, equal-weight 75 atoms, or a period-156 theta2 payload.
Treat cyclotomic-unit containment and regulator-level formulas as support until
an exact p25 selector and finite theorem are supplied.

The external distribution-relation scout keeps the same boundary for modern
distribution/Eisenstein-class citations. KL generator and distribution theory
is a valid framework source, but it is still support unless upgraded to the
exact p25 selector, an arithmetic source theorem, and scalar/branch
normalization.

## Open Blockers

- No theorem-legal mixed `C_3 x C_169` KL/Siegel/Kronecker product emitting the
  p25 selector is in hand.
- Generic modular-unit generation, raw exponent balance, and prime-power
  `C_169` projections are too broad for the current certificate target.
- Broad KL generator/congruence citations are repair rows unless they identify
  the exact primitive word, orientation, or theta2 payload.
- Cyclotomic-unit containment, Robert-unit norms, and regulator-level formulas
  still lack the exact p25 selector, finite row theorem, or exact-P payload.
- Modern distribution/Eisenstein-class citations remain support unless they
  add p25 selector, finite source theorem, and scalar/branch normalization.
