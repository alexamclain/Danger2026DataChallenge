# Sprang

## Purpose

Record the exact p25 boundary for the Sprang / Kronecker / D=2 source family.

## Current Claim

Sprang stays alive only as a possible exact specialization source. The current
papers contribute real D=2 Poincare/Kronecker/theta support: the 1801 source
explicitly avoids the classical coprime-to-6 obstruction at the
logarithmic-derivative/Kronecker-section level, and it supplies p-adic theta
and distribution-relation vocabulary.

That makes Sprang a serious support language for the exact-P/theta2 route, but
not the p25 closer as written.

The still-live hope is a named source theorem that emits:

```text
exact mixed source packet
+ T-translate orientation
+ K-traced P/theta2 or theta2-inverse payload
+ challenge-legal finite-field framing
```

Broad kernel-distribution or cohomology readings are already screened out.
The new theta2 source-intake scan keeps the same verdict in sharper form:
Sprang supports D=2 theta machinery, but the local arXiv sources do not name
the exact p25 theta2/theta2-inverse divisor-additive payload, the compact
`C,D,K,orientation` packet, the `Norm_156(Y_507)` bridge, or period-156
branch/root/telescoping data.

The distribution-instantiation falsifier rules out the strongest naive
upgrade: Sprang's distribution theorem is a full kernel/torsion trace identity,
while the p25 theta2 interface needs the sparse quotient packet or
`base*K_trace*D_segment*(1-T)` factor tuple. A future Sprang route must supply
that sparse selector, not just cite the full distribution relation.

The constructive-payload scan adds the packet-level version of that verdict:
the local Sprang texts have Kronecker-theta, p-adic theta, distribution,
de Rham, and Kato-Siegel support vocabulary, but no exact theta2/theta2-inverse
p25 payload, compact KSY packet, period-156 branch data, or evaluable p25 row.

The KL source-split local scan also checks the sharper exact-P/KL primitive
word hook against the Sprang TeX sources. They contain theta/Kronecker,
distribution, de Rham, and Eisenstein support language, but not the exact
primitive word, H90 source chain, boundary step, or K-trace/theta2 bridge.

## Open Blockers

- No exact D=2 finite product/value theorem for the p25 payload is in hand.
- The current literature gives D=2 theta/Kronecker support unless a sharper
  specialization appears.
- No Sprang source clause currently emits the accepted theta2/theta2-inverse
  divisor-additive payload or branch-selected p25 value.
- Sprang's full distribution relation still needs a sparse selector to become
  the p25 theta2 packet.
- The local sources also lack the constructive p25 payload hooks needed for
  packet intake: exact theta2 payload, compact KSY packet, period-156 branch
  data, or an evaluable p25 row.
- Generic splitting/diagonal language in the de Rham source is not the p25
  Q diagonal plus pure quartic split.
