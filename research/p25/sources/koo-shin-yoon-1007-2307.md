# Koo-Shin-Yoon 1007.2307

## Purpose

Capture exactly what the KSY paper contributes to the p25 exact-product route.

## Current Claim

KSY `1007.2307` is live as normalized-y vocabulary, not as a solved exact-P
theorem.

The useful pieces are:

- Equation (3.4): exact normalized-y atom formula.
- Theorem 5.3, Theorem 6.2, and Corollary 6.4: ray-class generation and
  single-value generator structure.

What is still missing is the exact 75-atom finite product identity, with mixed
graph, equal weights, orientation, and period-156 boundary.

The v2 exact-P contract now sharpens that missing object to a compact
`C,D,K,orientation` packet or an equivalent period-156 theta2
divisor/additive payload.

The constructive-payload scan confirms that the local TeX source has
normalized-y/ray-class/Siegel vocabulary but no compact `C,D,K,orientation`
packet, exact 75-atom theorem, period-156 bridge, or packetizable p25 payload.

The KL source-split local scan confirms the same status under the sharper KL
primitive-word hook: the local TeX has helper vocabulary, but no exact
primitive word, H90 source chain, boundary step, or K-trace/theta2 bridge.

## Open Blockers

- No exact product/distribution theorem selecting all 75 atoms is in hand.
- Field generation and single-value generators do not by themselves solve the
  DANGER3 payload.
- The normalized-y `X_{1,4}(N)` surface is useful vocabulary, but the missing
  object is still a finite selector/product theorem over the mixed payload.
- The local TeX source does not currently supply the additive-normalization
  closer demanded by the v2 H0/conductor-39 contract.
- The local TeX source also does not supply the constructive exact-P hooks:
  compact `C,D,K,orientation`, exact 75-atom theorem, or period-156 bridge.
- Its `Q` notation is a collision with the conductor-39 `Q` route, not a hook
  for that route.
